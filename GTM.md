# GTM

## Positioning

Snaelda is a small, curated website tool for owner-led businesses that want a proper landing page without getting trapped in a heavy builder.

This is not a mass-market launch.

The early goal is:

- make the product fun to try
- get people to value fast
- convert a small, opinionated customer base
- cap the first wave at 50 paying users

## Target User

Best-fit users:

- solo operators
- tiny shops
- local service businesses
- small creative businesses
- people who need a proper website quickly

Examples:

- knitting store
- yarn dyer
- ceramics studio
- florist
- photographer
- yoga teacher
- therapist
- bakery
- wedding planner

## Core Offer

The product promise is:

- prompt a site from the landing page
- get a good first draft immediately
- tweak it in a lightweight builder
- publish fast
- connect your own domain

The big GTM hook is:

`Prompt in. Good first draft out.`

## Funnel

### 1. Homepage prompt

The homepage should let users try the product immediately.

They can:

- enter a prompt
- generate a website draft
- preview it
- make a few light edits
- use up to 5 free prompts or re-prompts

No signup should be required before they see value.

### 2. Re-prompt loop

After the first draft, users will usually want to steer the result.

That is a feature, not a problem.

So the free experience should allow:

- first draft generation
- up to 5 total prompt actions
- prompt-based iteration from inside the builder

Examples:

- make it warmer
- add pricing
- remove a section
- change the tone
- swap the hero image direction

There is no hard time cutoff for now. The main free boundary is prompt count plus locked publishing.

### 3. Paid conversion

To publish, connect a domain, and continue prompting freely, the user subscribes.

## Pricing

Keep launch pricing extremely simple.

### Free

- generate a draft
- preview it
- light editing
- up to 5 prompts
- no publish
- no custom domain

### Founding 50

- `$20/month`
- or `$180/year`
- first 20 get 50% off the first year

That means:

- first 20 annual users pay `$90` for year one
- everyone after that pays the standard Founding 50 price
- once 50 paid users are in, signup closes

Includes:

- publish live
- custom domain connection
- forms
- SEO defaults
- lightweight analytics
- ongoing prompting
- 1 site
- up to 10 pages

## Capacity Model

This first version is intentionally capped.

- maximum 50 paying users
- once 50 are in, new subscriptions are closed
- after that, new people go to a waitlist or closed state

This should be visible on the landing page as a live capacity indicator.

Example messaging:

- `Founding 50`
- `17 spots left`
- `First 20 get 50% off the first year`
- `When it hits zero, we close the gate for now`

## Why This Model

This fits the product and the way it is being built:

- solo-dev friendly
- easier support load
- more personal user base
- stronger early feedback loop
- scarcity is real, not fake

At 50 users:

- `$20/month` = `$1,000 MRR`
- enough to justify the time
- still small enough to stay curated

## Launch Messaging

Keep the tone simple and direct.

Good framing:

- `Try it free with 5 prompts.`
- `Founding 50 is open.`
- `First 20 get 50% off the first year.`
- `Once the 50 spots are gone, new signups close for now.`
- `A proper first draft for small businesses that would rather get online than learn a giant website builder.`

## What Not To Do

- do not create multiple plans at launch
- do not make the free experience too limited before value is shown
- do not fake scarcity
- do not allow unlimited paid growth before the product is ready

## Recommendation

Launch with:

- homepage prompt
- free draft generation
- 5 free prompts
- one paid plan
- first 20 get 50% off the first year
- Founding 50 cap
- visible countdown on the landing page

That keeps the whole GTM motion coherent, lightweight, and believable.
