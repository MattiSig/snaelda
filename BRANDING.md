# Snaelda Branding Guide

## Brand Summary

`snaelda.io` should feel:

- warm
- quirky
- friendly
- lightweight
- a little silly
- still dependable

This is not a cold enterprise platform and not a loud hype machine. It should feel like a charming, capable little tool that helps people get a real website live without friction.

## Core Product Story

Snaelda helps users:

- spin up a landing page from a prompt
- edit it in a lightweight builder/CMS
- publish it fast
- attach their own domain
- get a clean, fast public website
- get SEO-friendly pages out of the box

The emotional promise is:

`serious result, un-serious process`

The tool should feel fun and approachable while still producing polished, credible websites.

## Target Customer

Snaelda is primarily for small, owner-led businesses and solo operators who need a simple website without turning it into a project.

The sweet spot is:

- a person or very small team
- selling a local service, small shop, class, craft, or creative practice
- needs a proper landing page or small site
- wants something warm and credible
- does not want to learn a giant website builder

Good examples:

- a knitting store
- a yarn dyer
- a ceramics studio
- a florist
- a small cafe
- a photographer
- a yoga teacher
- a therapist
- a dog groomer
- a wedding planner
- a local bakery
- a tiny design studio
- a tattoo artist
- a craft workshop host
- a music teacher

This is not primarily for:

- large companies
- complex SaaS products
- heavy e-commerce catalogs
- enterprise marketing teams
- advanced CMS-heavy businesses

The tone should always feel like it understands the user is trying to get a good small business website live, not launch a complicated digital platform.

## Brand Personality

Snaelda is:

- clever, not smug
- playful, not childish
- simple, not empty
- calm, not sterile
- helpful, not bossy

If the product had a voice, it would sound like:

- “let’s get this live”
- “that’s good enough to publish”
- “here, I made you a solid first draft”

Not:

- overly technical
- startup-bro loud
- luxury design precious
- robotic

## Name Feel

`snaelda` feels unusual, soft, hand-made, and slightly odd in a good way. Lean into that.

The brand should feel:

- woven
- crafted
- a bit magical
- personal

That matches the logo well.

## Logo Direction

The current logo has a watercolor spinning top wrapped in colorful ribbon-like strands. It feels:

- handcrafted
- kinetic
- playful
- intricate
- warm

This is a strong signal for the rest of the brand.

Interpretation:

- the spinning top suggests motion, energy, and making something go live
- the wrapped strands suggest threads, weaving, connection, and assembled parts
- the watercolor style keeps it human and soft instead of technical and rigid

The product UI and copy should support that feeling rather than fighting it.

## Visual Direction

### Overall Look

The brand should feel light, tactile, and cheerful.

Prefer:

- warm off-whites
- soft color fields
- gentle contrast
- rounded shapes
- hand-touched accents
- airy layouts

Avoid:

- hard black-on-white starkness
- ultra-corporate blues
- too much gloss or futurism
- heavy dark-mode-first branding
- overly minimal “blank SaaS” aesthetics

### Color Direction

Keep the logo as the emotional source, but use the Stitch design system as the current product palette baseline.

Logo cues:

- sky blue
- soft teal
- coral pink
- warm gold
- violet
- warm wood neutrals

The current product theme in Stitch shifts those cues into a warmer, denser working palette:

- deep plum-black foundations
- antique paper light surfaces
- saffron orange as the main action color
- soft teal as the steady secondary accent
- berry red for critical emphasis
- pale mauve and ribbon tints for selected or elevated states

Suggested brand behavior:

- warm neutral or plum-biased base
- one dominant action accent plus one supporting ribbon accent
- colorful illustrations/details
- restrained product surfaces, even when marketing can be more playful

### Core Palette

Primary working palette, aligned with the Stitch project `Snaelda Prompt-to-Site Builder`:

- `Plum Black` `#2D1B2D`
- `Night Workshop` `#131411`
- `Antique Paper` `#F9F7F2`
- `Warm Pebble` `#E5E0D8`
- `Thread Mauve` `#DABED6`
- `Saffron Orange` `#F4A261`
- `Soft Teal` `#2A9D8F`
- `Berry Red` `#E76F51`

Usage guidance:

- `Plum Black` and `Night Workshop` should anchor dark-mode surfaces
- `Antique Paper` and `Warm Pebble` should carry light-mode surfaces
- `Thread Mauve` is the preferred selected-state, focus-glow, and surface-tint color
- `Saffron Orange` is the main CTA and momentum color
- `Soft Teal` should support success, secondary emphasis, and focus treatments
- `Berry Red` is reserved for destructive actions, warnings, and rare punch accents

Supporting ribbon colors from the logo can still appear in illustration, onboarding, and marketing details:

- `Sky Ribbon` `#6CA9E8`
- `Coral Ribbon` `#F07A98`
- `Golden Ribbon` `#E5A13A`
- `Violet Ribbon` `#7A4CB0`
- `Wood` `#B78656`

### Light Mode

Light mode should stay chill.

Think:

- creamy backgrounds
- soft contrast
- colorful accents
- plenty of air

Recommended light-mode behavior:

- background: `#F9F7F2`
- secondary surfaces: `#E5E0D8` or warm off-white variants
- primary text: dark plum-brown, never pure black
- borders: faint mauve-brown neutrals, not cool gray
- CTA accents: `#F4A261` first, `#2A9D8F` second
- selected and focused states: mauve-tinted, not blue-tinted

### Dark Mode

Dark mode can be a bit meaner.

Not evil, just sharper and more dramatic.

Think:

- inky background
- stronger contrast
- brighter ribbons
- more electric accents

Recommended dark palette:

- `Night Workshop` `#131411`
- `Plum Black` `#2D1B2D`
- `Charcoal Moss` `#1F201D`
- `Thread Mauve` `#DABED6`
- `Saffron Orange` `#F4A261`
- `Soft Teal` `#2A9D8F`
- `Berry Red` `#E76F51`

Recommended dark-mode behavior:

- backgrounds should be near-black with a warm/plum bias, not flat neutral gray
- accent colors can be more saturated than in light mode
- cards should feel denser and punchier
- highlights can be a little smug, but the UI should still be readable and calm
- text should land in warm paper tones, not stark white
- mauve should do more of the selection and elevation work than blue

### Color System Rules

Keep the system disciplined:

- one base neutral family
- two main accent families active at once
- one loud accent reserved for emphasis
- theme tokens can shift tone, but the product brand should still feel recognizably Snaelda
- avoid pure black, pure white, and default cool grays
- product UI should usually lead with saffron plus teal, while mauve carries structure and focus

### Shape Language

Prefer:

- rounded corners
- soft shadows
- pill buttons
- curved dividers
- layered cards

The product should feel a little bouncy, not boxy.

### Texture and Illustration

The logo supports a lightly illustrated world.

Good supporting directions:

- soft gradients
- watercolor-inspired accents
- ribbon/thread motifs
- tiny confetti/spark details used sparingly

Do not overdo it. The product still needs to feel clean and usable.

## Typography Direction

Typography should feel friendly and readable first.

Prefer:

- `Be Vietnam Pro` for UI and body copy
- `Literata` for display headlines, section headers, and other brand-forward moments

The type should not feel too geometric or too luxury-editorial. Slight personality is good; novelty is not.

Usage guidance:

- use `Be Vietnam Pro` anywhere the product needs speed, clarity, and compact readability
- use `Literata` selectively to give the brand a bookish, hand-touched voice
- avoid pairing Stitch's warmer palette with cold neo-grotesk defaults

## Voice and Tone

### Writing Style

Write like a helpful creative tool, not a corporate platform.

Use:

- short, clear sentences
- light humor in small doses
- plain language
- encouraging framing

Avoid:

- buzzwords
- sounding “AI magical” every sentence
- overexplaining
- fake excitement

### Tone Examples

Good:

- `Start with a prompt. Tweak what matters. Publish when it feels right.`
- `A good-looking first draft in a minute beats a blank canvas for an hour.`
- `Your site is live. Nicely spun.`

Bad:

- `Revolutionize your digital presence with cutting-edge AI experiences.`
- `The future of intelligent website generation is here.`

## Messaging Pillars

### 1. Fast To First Draft

You can go from idea to website quickly.

### 2. Easy To Tweak

You are not trapped in generated output. You can edit, swap, refine, and publish.

### 3. Lightweight But Real

This is not a toy mockup tool. The output is a real website with real hosting and real domains.

### 4. SEO-Friendly By Default

The default output should already be good web hygiene:

- fast page loads
- server-served HTML
- clean metadata
- sensible headings
- indexable pages

Users should not need to be SEO experts to launch something solid.

### 5. Friendly By Default

The product lowers the intimidation barrier for getting online.

## Product Positioning

Snaelda sits somewhere between:

- AI website generator
- lightweight website builder
- small-business landing page tool

But it should present itself more simply:

`Make a simple website. Fast. Then make it yours.`

Expanded promise:

`Make a simple website. Fast. Then make it yours. Clean, quick, and SEO-friendly from day one.`

## UX Brand Rules

The product experience should feel:

- quick to understand
- low-stakes
- visually friendly
- never cluttered

UI copy should avoid making users feel like they need design or technical expertise.

Good product moments:

- friendly empty states
- simple publishing language
- reassuring domain connection flow
- small delightful touches, not noisy gimmicks

## Domain + Publishing Framing

This is an important part of the brand promise.

Users should understand:

- Snaelda gets them online quickly
- they can polish the page in a lightweight editor
- they can connect their own domain when ready

Recommended framing:

- `Start on a Snaelda URL. Bring your own domain when you're ready.`
- `Draft fast. Publish fast. Point your domain when it feels like home.`
- `Publish something fast, clean, and search-friendly without wrestling with a giant website builder.`

## Brand Do / Don't

Do:

- stay warm
- stay human
- stay simple
- keep a wink of silliness
- make the product feel light and capable

Don't:

- turn the brand into generic SaaS minimalism
- overbrand every screen with loud color
- make the copy too cute to be clear
- make the UI feel childish

## One-Line Brand Prompt

`Snaelda is a warm, quirky website-making tool that helps people spin up simple landing pages, tweak them in a lightweight builder, and connect their own domain when they’re ready.`
